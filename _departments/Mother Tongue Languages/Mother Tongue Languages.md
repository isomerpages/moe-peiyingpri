<h4>MTL Fortnight</h4>
<p>We aim to provide platforms for pupils to actively learn Mother Tongue
Languages and the associated culture so as to experience Mother Tongue
Languages as ‘living languages’</p>
<p>Students are immersed in a variety of level appropriate activities throughout
the two weeks.</p>
<p>These include singing competitions, chinese calligraphy workshops, dikir
barat, mask making, learning journey’s to the Indian Heritage Centre and
e-book competitions.</p>
<h4>P3 Mother Tongue Languages and Cultural Day Camp</h4>
<p>Primary 3 pupils were provided with the opportunity to immerse in their
respective Mother Tongue Languages, and to appreciate the cultures through
hands-on tasks such as drama, performances and story-telling.</p>
<h4>MTL Fun Reader Programme(FRP)“Nurturing Minds through Fun Reading</h4>
<p>We aim to:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Build MTL reading culture through engaging reading activities</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Empower students to become proficient readers and lifelong learners in
their MTL</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Enhance critical thinking , communication skills, and creativity in students</p>
</li>
</ul>
<h4>MTL ePride Project – Applied Learning Programme (ALP) Collaboration with the Science Department</h4>
<p>We aim to:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Provide graduating P6 students with the opportunity to manifest the PRIDE
values through heartwarming and personalized stories written and produced
as e-books.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Develop respectful communication skills to build relationships while campaigning
for environmental conservation.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Use an e-book production application to create interactive e-books that
support self-directed learning.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Utilize the e-books as CCE teaching and learning resources for lower primary
students.</p>
</li>
</ul>
